Why clinics lose money from inactive patients

Often these patients are not unhappy — they simply forget to book, delay their visit, or never get around to returning. The clinic has already spent time and money acquiring them, so every inactive patient is a missed revenue opportunity.

The problem

Patients forget to rebook, hygiene visits get delayed, and checkups fall through the cracks. Over time, this can leave a large number of patients inactive.

The opportunity

A short, well-structured campaign can bring a percentage of those patients back into the practice and help fill underused appointment slots.

How the campaign works

The structure is simple, easy to understand, and focused on one clear goal: getting inactive patients to come back.

1

Identify inactive patients

Build a list of patients who have not visited within the last 6–12 months.

2

Send a short sequence

Use a concise, friendly reminder campaign inviting them to book a hygiene or checkup appointment.

3

Turn replies into bookings

Interested patients are directed back to the clinic to schedule their visit and return to active care.

Example reactivation message

This is the kind of message a clinic could send to inactive patients.

Subject: Quick reminder from the clinic
Hi,

Our records show you may be due for a dental checkup or hygiene appointment.

We currently have a few appointments available this month if you'd like to book.

If you'd like to schedule a visit, our team would be happy to help.
